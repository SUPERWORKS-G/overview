The three people in every case

Role 1 The Client

Needs a notarised document. Submits online. Pays a deposit. Gets WhatsApp updates at every step. Never sees anything outside Squarespace.

Role 2 The Notary

Subscribed to the platform. Receives structured, ready-to-work cases. Reviews documents, confirms appointments, updates status. Never touches the client-facing site.

Role 3 The Admin

Runs the platform. Onboards notary offices. Handles exceptions. Everything routine runs without them.

The six tools — and what each one does

Squarespace Client-facing

The website, the form, the client's case page. Everything the client sees.

Airtable Operations

The case database. Where notaries work. Where the admin oversees. Invisible to clients.

Zapier Automation

Connects everything. Watches for changes. Fires messages. No human needed.

Google Drive File storage

Stores all documents. Each case has its own folder. Notaries see only their cases.

Stripe Payments

Takes the deposit. Payment confirmation is what opens the case — automatically.

WhatsApp Notifications

Every status change sends a message to the client. Nothing sent manually.

1. The Client Journey

No account. No calls. No office visit — until the appointment is confirmed and the client is ready to sign.

What the client does — step by step

1
Fill in a short form
Name, document type, contact details. Two minutes. On acte.online/start.
2
Upload documents
Passport or national ID. Any supporting files. No Google account needed.
3
Pay the deposit
A fixed fee via Stripe. This is what opens the case. No payment — no case.
4
Choose preferred dates
First choice, second choice, preferred time of day. The notary confirms the exact slot.
5
Case is opened Automatic
Payment clears. Zapier creates the Airtable record, generates an access code, creates the Google Drive folder, sends the client a WhatsApp with their case link. Seconds.
6
Client receives their case page Automatic
A private link — no password to type. The page shows status, assigned notary, and appointment details. It updates as the case moves forward.
7
WhatsApp: case in preparation Automatic
Notary sets status to In Review. Client receives: "Your case is being prepared by [Office Name]."
8
WhatsApp: appointment confirmed Automatic
Notary confirms a slot. Client receives the date, time, and office address.
9
Physical appointment
Client attends. Signs. Pays the remaining balance to the office.
10
Documents delivered Automatic
Notary marks the case Completed. Client receives a WhatsApp with download links.

What the client never has to do

Create an account. Remember a password. Call the office. Chase a status update. Re-upload anything. It is all handled.

The client's case page

Every client gets one private Squarespace page. It lives at a URL like:

acte.online/case-001234?password=ABC12345

The password is embedded in the link. The client clicks and lands directly on their page — no typing, no login. It updates as the case moves from Submitted to Completed.

2. The Notary Journey

Notaries are the paying subscribers. Cases arrive structured and complete — documents uploaded, deposit paid, preferred dates selected. They review, prepare, and confirm. The back-and-forth is already done. Their working tool is Airtable. The client-facing site is just the front door.

Case lifecycle — what notaries see

01 Submitted
02 In Review
03 Booked
04 Completed

The notary changes the status. Each change fires a WhatsApp to the client — automatically.

The notary workflow — step by step

1
New case arrives

WhatsApp notification: case ID, document type, client name. Notary logs into the Squarespace Member Area.

2
See their cases — and only their cases

One click from the portal. A filtered view opens showing only their assigned cases — client details, preferred dates, uploaded documents. Nothing from other offices. Nothing extraneous.

3
Review and set status to In Review

Notary checks documents, begins preparation, updates Status. Client is notified by WhatsApp immediately. No action needed from the notary.

4
Confirm the appointment

Notary picks a slot from the client's preferred dates. Fills in Booking Date and Booking Time. Client receives a WhatsApp with the date, time, and address.

5
Set status to Booked

Appointment locked. Client notified. Notary prepares the notarial act.

6
Mark as Completed

After the appointment, notary uploads prepared documents to Google Drive and sets Status to Completed. Client receives download links by WhatsApp. Case closed.

What the notary never has to do

Send a manual message. Call the client. Chase uploads. The routine communication runs itself.

3. The Admin Journey

The admin runs the platform. Most of it runs itself. The admin focuses on onboarding new offices, handling exceptions, and keeping things healthy.

What the admin does — daily

1
Sort incoming uploads

Client documents land in /Incoming-Uploads/. Admin moves them to the correct case folder. Automated at higher volume.

2
Check for stalled cases

Filter Airtable for cases unchanged in 5+ days. Follow up with the relevant notary office.

3
Check Zapier for failures

Zapier logs every run. If something failed, fix and re-run manually.

4
Respond to support

Clients email support@acte.online. Most questions are about case status — a quick Airtable check is enough.

What the admin does — per new case

1
Verify automation ran

After a new payment, confirm Airtable record exists, WhatsApp was sent, Google Drive folders are in place.

2
Create the client case page

Duplicate the Squarespace template. Set the URL to the Case ID. Set the password to the Access Code. Publish. Automated at 30+ cases/month.

3
Grant Google Drive access

Share the case folder with the assigned notary office. Viewer access — they can read, not delete.

What the admin does — monthly

1
Invoice notary offices

By the 1st of each month. Update Subscription Status in Airtable when payment arrives.

2
Check Zapier task usage

750 tasks/month on the Starter plan. Upgrade to Professional before the limit is hit.

3
Confirm GDPR deletions ran

Zapier deletes cases older than 12 months every night. Check the Deletion Audit table to confirm.

Onboarding a new notary office

1
Collect details and sign a DPA

Office name, email, city, subscription tier. Data Processing Agreement signed before any case data is shared.

2
Set up Airtable view

Create a filtered view showing only their cases. Share the link. Paste it into their record in Airtable.

3
Add to Squarespace Member Area

Add their email as a member. They receive an automatic invitation to set a password.

4
Welcome message and a 20-minute call

Walk through the Member Area, the Airtable view, and a sample case. Set expectations for response times and support.

4. How the Automation Works

Six workflows run in the background. None need a human to trigger. They fire in response to events.

What triggers what

Event
What happens automatically
Client pays the deposit
Airtable case record created. Google Drive folders created. Client Access Code generated. WhatsApp sent to client with their case link. Fallback email sent.

Airtable record deleted. Google Drive folder deleted. Deletion logged. Runs every night at 02:00.

The key insight

The notary changes one field — Status. That single action sends the right WhatsApp to the client, at the right moment, with the right content. The notary never thinks about notifications.

WhatsApp vs. email

WhatsApp is primary. Email is the fallback — sent alongside every WhatsApp.

Email lands in spam. For Romanian clients — at home and abroad — WhatsApp is the dominant channel. Messages are read. Instantly.
