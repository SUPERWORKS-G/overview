The problem

Getting a document notarised is harder than it should be.

Whether you call ahead or walk in, the experience is largely the same. You explain your situation from scratch. You're told what documents are needed — documents you didn't bring. You come back. You wait. No one tells you where things stand or when to expect a result. You follow up. Sometimes you follow up again.

For walk-ins specifically, the friction starts before you even sit down. Office hours are fixed. Queues are unpredictable. The notary may be with another client, in court, or unavailable that day. You've taken time off work and may leave with nothing resolved.

For notary offices, the picture is just as fragmented. Requests arrive by phone, email, and walk-in — each in a different format, each missing something. There is no single record. Time is spent chasing documents, calling clients to update them, and managing no-shows from people who were never committed in the first place.

The solution

Everything done before the first meeting. Everything running after it.

The intake moves online. The client submits their request, uploads documents, and pays a deposit before any notary time is spent. By the time a case reaches the notary, it is complete and ready to work. No back-and-forth. No missing files. No uncommitted clients.

Updates go out automatically at each stage. The client always knows where things stand — without calling, visiting, or waiting for someone to reply.

1. The Client Journey

No account. No calls. No office visit — until the appointment is confirmed and the client is ready to sign.

The intake form includes city and notary office selection. City is picked first; this filters the office list to show only those operating in that city. The client can then pick a specific office or leave it to the platform. One form. Everything in one place.

What the client does — step by step

1
Fill in a short form
Name, document type, contact details. City and preferred notary office. Two minutes. On acte.online/start.
2
Upload documents
Passport or national ID. Any supporting files. No Google account needed.
3
Pay the deposit
A fixed fee via Stripe. This is what opens the case. No payment — no case.
4
Choose preferred dates
First choice, second choice, preferred time of day. The notary confirms the exact slot.
5
Case is opened Automatic
Payment clears. Case record created, access code generated, Google Drive folder created, WhatsApp sent to client with their case link. Seconds.
6
Notary office confirmed Automatic
If the client picked an office, it is assigned as requested. If not, the admin assigns the best available one in the chosen city. Either way, the client receives a WhatsApp: "Your case has been assigned to [Office Name]." No surprises. No overrides without the client's consent.
7
Client receives their case page link Automatic
A private link sent by WhatsApp. No password to type. The page is created and updated manually by the admin — the WhatsApp is only sent once the page is ready. The client always lands on a current page.
8
WhatsApp: case in preparation Automatic
Notary sets status to In Review. Client receives: "Your case is being prepared by [Office Name]."
9
WhatsApp: appointment confirmed Automatic
Notary confirms a slot. Client receives the date, time, and office address.
10

Client attends. Signs. Pays the remaining balance to the office.
11
Documents delivered Automatic
Notary marks the case Completed. Client receives a WhatsApp with download links.

City and notary office — part of the form

The intake form includes two linked fields. First: which city? Once selected, a second field appears showing only the offices operating there. The client picks one — or selects "No preference, assign one for me."

If a specific office is chosen, that choice is honoured. The admin does not reassign without going back to the client first — especially relevant when the choice is based on a recommendation or past experience. If the preferred office is unavailable, the admin contacts the client by WhatsApp and agrees on an alternative before anything changes.

Both fields are native Squarespace conditional logic. No code needed.

What the client never has to do

Create an account. Remember a password. Call the office. Chase a status update. Re-upload anything. It is all handled.

The client's case page

Every client gets one private page. It lives at a URL like:

acte.online/case-001234?password=ABC12345

The password is embedded in the link. The client clicks and lands directly on their page — no typing, no login.

The page is static — updated manually by the admin each time the case moves forward. To ensure the client never lands on a stale page, the WhatsApp message is only sent after the admin has updated the page. The admin uses a simple checklist field to confirm the page is ready before the notification goes out.

2. The Notary Journey

Notaries are the paying subscribers. Cases arrive structured and complete — city selected, documents uploaded, deposit paid, preferred dates chosen. The client has either picked the office by name or been assigned to it by the platform. Either way, by the time the case lands, the prep is done. Their working tool is Airtable. The client-facing site is just the front door.

Case lifecycle — what notaries see

01 Submitted
02 In Review
03 Booked
04 Completed

The notary changes the status. Each change fires a WhatsApp to the client — automatically.

The notary workflow — step by step

1
New case arrives

WhatsApp notification: case ID, document type, client name. Notary logs into the Squarespace Member Area.

2
See their cases — and only their cases

One click from the portal. A filtered view opens showing only their assigned cases — client details, preferred dates, uploaded documents. Nothing from other offices. Nothing extraneous.

3
Review and set status to In Review

Notary checks documents, begins preparation, updates Status. The admin is signalled to update the client's case page. Once done, the WhatsApp goes out. No action needed from the notary.

4
Confirm the appointment

Notary picks a slot from the client's preferred dates. Fills in Booking Date and Booking Time. Client receives a WhatsApp with the date, time, and address.

5
Set status to Booked

Appointment locked. Client notified. Notary prepares the notarial act.

6
Mark as Completed

After the appointment, notary uploads prepared documents to Google Drive and sets Status to Completed. Client receives download links by WhatsApp. Case closed.

What the notary never has to do

Send a manual message. Call the client. Chase uploads. The routine communication runs itself.

3. The Admin Journey

The admin runs the platform. Most of it runs itself. The admin focuses on onboarding new offices, handling exceptions, and keeping things healthy.

What the admin does — daily

1
Sort incoming uploads

Client documents land in /Incoming-Uploads/. Admin moves them to the correct case folder. Automated at higher volume.

2
Check for stalled cases

Filter Airtable for cases unchanged in 5+ days. Follow up with the relevant notary office.

3
Check Zapier for failures

Zapier logs every run. If something failed, fix and re-run manually.

4
Respond to support

Clients email support@acte.online. Most questions are about case status — a quick Airtable check is enough.

What the admin does — per new case

1
Verify automation ran

After a new payment, confirm Airtable record exists, WhatsApp was sent, Google Drive folders are in place.

2
Create the client case page

Duplicate the Squarespace template. Set the URL to the Case ID. Set the password to the Access Code. Publish. Automated at 30+ cases/month.

3
Update the client case page on status change

When the notary changes status, the admin updates the case page to reflect it — new status, appointment details, or download links. Once updated, the admin checks a "Page ready" field. Zapier watches this field and fires the WhatsApp to the client only then. The client never lands on a stale page.

4
Grant Google Drive access

Share the case folder with the assigned notary office. Viewer access — they can read, not delete.

What the admin does — monthly

1
Invoice notary offices

By the 1st of each month. Update Subscription Status in Airtable when payment arrives.

2
Check Zapier task usage

750 tasks/month on the Starter plan. Upgrade to Professional before the limit is hit.

3
Confirm GDPR deletions ran

Zapier deletes cases older than 12 months every night. Check the Deletion Audit table to confirm.

Onboarding a new notary office

1
Collect details and sign a DPA

Office name, email, city, subscription tier. Data Processing Agreement signed before any case data is shared.

2
Set up Airtable view

Create a filtered view showing only their cases. Share the link. Paste it into their record in Airtable.

3
Add to Squarespace Member Area

Add their email as a member. They receive an automatic invitation to set a password.

4
Welcome message and a 20-minute call

Walk through the Member Area, the Airtable view, and a sample case. Set expectations for response times and support.

4. How the Automation Works

Six workflows run in the background. None need a human to trigger. They fire in response to events.

The key insight

The notary changes one field — Status. That signals the admin to update the client's case page. Once the page is ready, the WhatsApp fires. The client always lands on a current page. The notary never thinks about any of it.

WhatsApp vs. email

WhatsApp is primary. Email is the fallback — sent alongside every WhatsApp.

Email lands in spam. For Romanian clients — at home and abroad — WhatsApp is the dominant channel. Messages are read. Instantly.
